Biography

Early Life

Deem was raised in Indiana and later moved to New York City where she stayed as part of the art scene for 12 years1.

In 1970 while pursuing an Art supervision degree, Deem visited Chicago where she saw her first hologram1,2.

On her return to New York, she pursued a course in holography at the New York School of Holography1.

Pulse Ruby Laser

Developed the pulse ruby laser system over several years in Germany.

Allowed the recording of live subjects in holograms.

Produced one of the first reduced pulse portrait holograms published as an embossed printed hologram3.

The Shearwater Foundation's Holography Award

Awarded to holographers who had made important contributions to holography who had been working in the field for at least 5 years.

In 1988, Rebecca Deem was one of six holographers awarded this prize along with $10,0004.

Now

Deem currently lives in Santa Fe with her husband and fellow holographer, Fred Unterseher.

Artwork

Pulse Laser Hologram of President Ronald Reagan

This was the "first pulsed laser portrait of a president for the Smithsonian Portrait Collection"5.

This hologram was created in conjunction with several other holographers: Fred Unterseher, Hans Bjelkhagen, John Landry, and Michael Marhic1,6.
